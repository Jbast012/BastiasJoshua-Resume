My name is Joshua Bastias, I am a 3rd-year psychology student at the Univeristy of California, Riverside.

I have an extensive background working with children, which started when I turned 16 and got my first job coaching tumbling. This job was where I learned that I enjoyed coaching and working with children. It is also where I began learning to connect and educate children. After highschool I continued to coach gymnastics at the YMCA in Lakewood. Here I learned more about coaching competitive gymnastics and was quickly trusted to coach higher level athletes with much more complex skills. I also learned about Social Emotional Learning and implemented it into what I was teaching the athletes. After over half a year with the YMCA I continued my professional jouney volunteering under a Psychiatric Social Worker at my Alma Mater, Nathaniel Narbonne Senior High. Here I was helping to continue leading the students who were involved in the Mental Health Awareness Club which I founded and left behind. I worked one on one with students who held leadership positions in the club to learn more about how to be an effective leader, and how to effectively run a club as I had done years prior. Among my work as a volunteer I also worked with student out reach and retention for the club. I would have one on one conversations which students to see why they had been absent from the club for some time and woudl help them find solutions to any issues they were facing so they can return to the club an become an active member again. This was crucial as the Mental Health Awareness Club was known for hosting huge events on campus surrounding mindfulness, kidness, social acitvisim, and student advocacy. The more studnets that were active members of the club, the more hands we had on deck to create bigger an better events.
